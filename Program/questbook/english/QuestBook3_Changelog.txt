#QUEST	Tutorial_Fight_Info

Reference: The combat system of the game

#TEXT	1

THE COMBAT SYSTEM OF THE GAME

*

Masterly possession of a blade and a pistol will allow I to get out of any scrape alive. The combat system provides I with three types of attack strikes, two types of parry, block, one circular strike for defense and attack, the ability to fire a pistol, bounce back and to the sides.

*

life

A character's life is the amount of damage they can take. Life increases with each new level, and can also be increased by additional abilities.

During the battle, your life is displayed with a red stripe in the upper right corner, to the left of the character's portrait. I can replenish your life during the battle using bottles of elixirs ("healers") through the Enter menu/Use or hotkey.

*

health

Health is an indicator of the overall physical condition of the hero. The worse the health, the worse the hero's performance in the game becomes. Health deteriorates if the hero is injured a lot, and recovers if the hero does not participate in hand-to-hand combat for a long time. If it falls below excellent, the hero begins to receive disadvantages in characteristics, and those, in turn, give disadvantages in skills. Health is restored by itself, quite slowly, but still. The fastest is when the hero is doing nothing (sleeping in a tavern), twice as slow while sailing on a ship. The "Excellent Health" ability speeds up the recovery of health by half. The golden background of the health indicator indicates that health has recovered to the maximum. However, the maximum health can also decrease when receiving serious damage (scars and mutilations, which even after full healing make themselves felt in battle). Priests in churches can restore maximum health, but infrequently, not much and for a lot of money. Sometimes it takes 2-3 priests to visit in order for "good golden" health to become "good white" - and still after that I have to wait for health to restore itself to "excellent golden".

*

energy

A man cannot fight without stopping, eventually he will be overcome by fatigue. The amount of energy depends on the character's "Reaction" characteristic, the higher the value, the more the character is capable of.  Each blow consumes a certain amount of energy, so I should use your strength very carefully in battle. The amount of energy is displayed by a blue bar to the right of the character's portrait. If there is not enough energy, I will not be able to strike. Energy is gradually restored during combat, if I see that there is very little energy left and there is not enough for strikes - try to block or parry the blows of enemies until the energy is restored.  Additional abilities will help I use energy more economically on strikes and restore it faster.

*

ENERGY AND LIFE OF ENEMIES

During the battle, stripes with their life and energy will hang over the heads of your enemies. When I or your allies strike an enemy, a number will fly off his head showing the amount of damage inflicted on him.

***

TYPES OF STRIKES

A slashing blow is a slashing blow with a swing, inflicts medium damage, requires a little energy.

*

A stabbing blow is a quick blow that causes minor damage. It requires more energy than chopping, but at the expense of speed it allows I to interrupt the opponent's swing.

*

A punching blow requires a lot of energy, is applied on a large scale, slowly, but causes very great damage, and always breaks through the block.

*

A circular strike requires an average amount of energy and causes minor damage to all the characters standing around. The character who missed the roundabout staggers back slightly, freeing up room for the hero to maneuver, and gives a break for a counterattack.   *

*

Parrying allows I to parry a blow, taking the opponent's saber aside, does not require

Energy, saves from a punchy blow. With a successful parry, the opponent loses his rhythm and spends most of his energy.

*

A feint looks the same as a punch. If I feint and the opponent strikes a blow, your character will intercept his saber and strike back at him. Using a feint does not waste energy, and a successful deceptive strike with damage takes very little. A feint allows I to intercept a punching blow.

*

The block allows I to block all punches except the puncher. He doesn't waste energy. I can leave the block at any time, interrupting your attack if it is immediately obvious that it will be unsuccessful. The block can sometimes be broken through with ordinary punches if the opponent has the appropriate abilities.

*

A pistol shot causes damage to the enemy and causes that

Take a step back. Pistols come in different types and are one-, two- and four-shot, bullets are needed to shoot, reloading pistols in battle depends on the game setting and is performed only with the saber removed or not.

*

The rebound allows I to bounce two steps away from the enemy back or to the side, thereby removing the hero from under attack or from the thick of the battle.

*

SETTING UP BUTTONS FOR STROKES

To facilitate control, the game has a kick mode button, pressing it gives a second mode for the button. By default, stabbing and circular are on the same button â€“ the left mouse button, punching and feint - the middle mouse button, chopping and parrying - the right mouse button. The block, shot and bounces are each on their own button.
